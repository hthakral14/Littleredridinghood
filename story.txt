STORY TITLE:
Little Red Riding Hood

MAIN CHARACTERS:
Little Red Riding Hood, Grandmother, Wolf, Woodsman

SETTING:
A small village near a forest and a cottage in the woods.

STORY:

1. A Girl named little Red Riding Hood who lived in a village near a forest.
2. She always wore a red hooded cloak made by her grandmother thats why she called little red riding hood.
3. Little Red Riding Hood loved visiting her grandmother, who lived alone in a cottage on the other side of the forest.
4. One morning, her mother packed a basket with muffins, honey, and herbal tea.
5. Her mother told her to take the basket to her grandmother and warned her to stay on the path and never talk to strangers.
6. Little Red Riding Hood promised her mother and started walking through the forest.
7. While walking, she met a wolf in the forest.
8. The wolf asked her where she was going.
9. Little Red Riding Hood told the wolf she was going to her grandmother’s house.
10. The wolf pretended to be kind and suggested she pick some flowers for her grandmother.
11. Little Red Riding Hood stopped and picked flowers in a sunny meadow.
12. While she was picking flowers, the wolf ran to her grandmother’s house.
13. The wolf knocked on the door, went inside, locked the grandmother in a closet, and dressed up like her.
14. Later, Little Red Riding Hood reached the cottage and went inside.
15. She noticed that her grandmother looked strange.
16. Little Red Riding Hood talked to the wolf, who was pretending to be her grandmother.
17. Suddenly, the wolf jumped out of bed and tried to attack Little Red Riding Hood.
18. At that moment, a woodsman came to the house.
19. The woodsman heard the noise, entered the cottage, scared the wolf away, and saved Little Red Riding Hood.
20. The woodsman freed the grandmother from the closet.
21. Little Red Riding Hood and her grandmother were safe again.

ENDING:
22. Little Red Riding Hood returned home safely with her grandmother.

LESSON:
23. Children should never talk to strangers and should always listen to their elders.
